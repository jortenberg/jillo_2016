I am an empathic designer. While forming creative solutions, I always strive to get inside the users head. What am I making? What is its purpose? Who am I making it for? What are the user’s expectations? How should it feel? The answers that come to light steer the course. The team and stakeholders on a project have so many different agendas and responsibilities. I always try to keep it simple and stand in the users shoes. I have a strong interest in coding alongside design. I am a graduate of the Web Development Immersive at General Assembly. It is awesome to be able to produce a finished product! and it was a great experience also. I am a dev-igner, skilled with Adobe CC, Sketch, HTML5, CSS/CSS3, JavaScript, JQuery, Node, and GreenSock Animation Platform (GSAP). Please view some of my work below.

Openhouser

Full Stack Web Development

Openhouser is a web application I created for real estate salespeople to manage open houses. A sign-in form generates a database of prospective clients. This database can be filtered in a number of ways to support the salesperson’s business. The app can also send automated or custom emails to any filtered list. This was my final project for the Web Development Immersive at General Assembly. Check out Openhouser and view the repository as well.

Toolbox

Ruby on Rails, PostgreSQL, HTLM5, CSS, Bootstrap

Bayer Aspirin

Advertising Campaign Concept

The creative brief said to develop ideas where the foundation was somehow distinct to the Bayer Aspirin brand. I was most interested in the fact that Bayer Aspirin had been relieving pain since 1898. The longest continuous brand history over all other aspirin. My idea was to create stories of historical figures or moments where Bayer Aspirin helped ease the pain. While researching I learned, the word Aspirin was actually Bayer's brand name. However, Bayer's rights to the trademark were lost or sold over time. For these comps I was responsible concept, art direction, design, copy, logo, tagline, and photo research.

Toolbox

Adobe Photoshop, Adobe InDesign, Adobe Illustrator

American Institute of Physics

Print Collateral Series

I was excited when asked to design a series of promotional brochures for the American Institute of Physics (AIP). The Brand Manager provided me with “spin-art” imagery to use as a unifying brand element for the series. I was also directed to use art from previously published journals. When I looked at the journals, the images were almost all charts and diagrams? The challenge was to create a look and feel for the series out of the info-graphics. There were roughly 20 different Journals published by AIP and I designed promo brochures for all of them. To the left is a sampling of some of their covers.

John Deere

Advertising Campaign Concept

I remember sitting at my desk, deadline looming, wishing an idea would come my way for John Deere equipment. Usually that is exactly the frame of mind that brings lame ideas. Great ideas generally come while driving down the highway on a Sunday, or in places like the shower. I was looking at photographs of all kinds of John Deere machinery and started thinking about how all the machines move and transform earth. These executions followed. Unfortunately, this campaign was not selected for production. I was responsible for concept, copy, art direction, and design.

Century 21

Advertising Campaign Concept

I was out doing exploratory research for Century 21, a.k.a, pretending to be a person shopping for a house. The realtor commented that housing prices have nearly doubled in the past several years. There was lots of snow on the day I went out to photograph the houses, which inspired the pleasing art direction. I also photographed small snippets of newsprint paper to make the classified ad clippings. I was responsible for concept, copy, art direction, and design.
